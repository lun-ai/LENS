**Summary of Key Components in the Prolog Program**  

This program analyzes electrical circuits by evaluating how gates (components) are connected and isolated. Here’s what the main parts do:  

1. **`isolated_behind(A, B)`**  
   Determines if gate **A** is "isolated behind" gate **B**. This is true if:  
   - **A** and **B** are the same gate, or  
   - All gates directly connected to **A** are *also* isolated behind **B**.  
   This helps identify dependencies where **A** cannot function independently of **B** in the circuit.  

2. **`partition(A, B, C)`**  
   Splits a group of gates (**A**) into two subgroups:  
   - **B**: Gates in the same circuit as **A** that *are* isolated behind others.  
   - **C**: Gates in the same circuit as **A** that *are not* isolated behind others.  
   This categorizes gates based on their isolation relationships.  

3. **`partition_sizes(A, B)`**  
   Calculates the number of gates in each subgroup created by `partition`.  
   - For example, if a group of 10 gates is split into subgroups of 4 and 6, the result is the pair `[4, 6]`.  

4. **`optimal_partition_sizes(A, B)`**  
   Compares different ways to split groups of gates and selects the **best split** based on a specific goal:  
   - It prioritizes splits where the *smaller subgroup* is as large as possible.  
   For instance, between splits `[4, 6]` and `[5, 5]`, it picks `[5, 5]` because the smaller subgroup (5) is larger than 4.  

**Purpose**  
The program helps optimize circuit design by identifying isolation patterns and selecting the most balanced way to divide components, ensuring efficiency or reliability in the circuit layout.
